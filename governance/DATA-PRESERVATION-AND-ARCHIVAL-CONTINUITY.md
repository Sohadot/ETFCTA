# Data Preservation and Archival Continuity

> A live link identifies a current location; a dated copy and hash identify what was actually reviewed.

Every admitted source MUST retain identity metadata, retrieval time, exact locator, preservation status, and SHA-256 for the reviewed bytes. A disappearing or replaced issuer file does not erase its source record. SEC accession metadata, reporting period, and filing form are preserved when applicable.

Protected or licensed content MUST NOT be republished merely because it was reviewed. ETFCTA separates public metadata, bounded rights-aware excerpts, original summaries, and restricted preserved bytes. A hash may remain public even when the preserved copy cannot be distributed.

Withdrawn and superseded records remain addressable and are excluded from current-state output. A complete export MUST include funds, sources, evidence, decisions, changes, rule versions, schemas, glossary, release metadata, and preservation manifests.

Continuity requires at least two independently recoverable copies before production admission, periodic restore tests, documented ownership of storage, and export formats that do not depend on a single vendor. Transfer to a future owner MUST preserve stable IDs, licenses, restrictions, audit history, and integrity hashes.
