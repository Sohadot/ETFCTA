# Source Identity Rules

A source is eligible for evidence extraction only when its origin, title, type, applicable date, retrieval date, and locator are independently identifiable. Authority labels describe provenance; they do not guarantee that every statement is sufficient for every classification.

The registry MUST distinguish publication date, effective date, reporting period, filing date, and retrieval date whenever those concepts differ. A replacement document creates a new source version; it does not mutate the preserved identity of the previous source.
