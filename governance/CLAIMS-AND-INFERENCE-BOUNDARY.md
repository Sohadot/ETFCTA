# Claims and Inference Boundary

This policy governs human pages, JSON, CSV, reports, metadata, search snippets, exports, and tool output.

| Class | Meaning | Publication requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Fact | Stable identity or directly verifiable event | Verified source and locator |
| Direct disclosure | What a source explicitly states | Source identity, bounded evidence, no stronger paraphrase |
| Derived classification | Result of a named ECS rule | Admitted evidence, rule/version, reasoning, limitations, review date |
| Analytical interpretation | Non-standard explanatory analysis | Labeled interpretation and linked evidence |
| Hypothesis | Testable but unverified proposition | Labeled hypothesis; never stored as current fund state |
| Unknown | Evidence does not establish an answer | Explicit unknown state and reviewed scope |
| Conflict | Admitted evidence disagrees | Conflict state; no silent resolution |
| Prohibited claim | Suitability, superiority, prediction, or unsupported causation | MUST NOT publish |

ETFCTA MUST NOT claim that a fund protects against crises, is better, will outperform, or is suitable for an investor. It MUST NOT call transparency “high” without a governed definition and evidence. A disclosure change MUST NOT be described as a fund or strategy change unless evidence supports the underlying change.

Visual hierarchy MUST NOT blur direct evidence and derived conclusions. Unknown does not mean absent; absence of disclosure does not prove absence of a property.
