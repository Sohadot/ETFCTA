Structural fingerprint.

A compact representation of classified structure and knowledge state. It is never a score.

What it can show

Confirmed primary disclosure, ECS-derived values, explicit unknowns, non-applicable dimensions, and conflicts.

What it cannot show

Quality, superiority, expected return, risk-adjusted merit, or investor suitability.

Current examples

KMLM and DBMF have different structural fingerprints because their disclosed construction differs—not because one ranks above the other.
