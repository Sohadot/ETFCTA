Change is recorded as an event.

Not a yearly snapshot. Every event distinguishes what changed from why our judgment changed.

  1. Fund changedThe underlying strategy or structure changed.
  2. Disclosure changedA source now describes the fund differently.
  3. Evidence improvedBetter evidence, without assuming a fund change.
  4. Rule changedA versioned ECS rule altered the result.
  5. ETFCTA correctionOur prior record was wrong and is corrected publicly.

Knowledge enters through a protocol.

Discovery is not publication. Every fact must cross the same ten-stage admission path before it can support a public classification.
